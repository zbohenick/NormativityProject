Normativity Data Visualization

Zach Bohenick

My Values

Balance

Providing time to relax, be mindful, and exercise to balance out work and school commitments.

Health

Prioritizing mental and physical health, as well as general well-being.

Creativity

Creating something original from the soul and/or intuition.

Knowledge

Learning new things and exercising the mind.

Stimulation

Engaging in fun and play, or anything exciting.

Balance

To record balance in my life, I divided my day into four categories: Work, Leisure, Relax, and Exercise. Every four hours, I recorded the percentage of time my actions fell into any of the four categories. On the chart, the center circle represents 10%. Each outward circle scales by 10%. I also recorded how I felt every four hours. I decided to pair these measurements with my health value. I found that balance affected my productivity, creativity, and well-being.

Health

Referencing the moods I recorded for my balance data, I also tracked moments of anxiety. I organized each feeling into two categories: positive and negative. The green shaded brain represent a positive mood, while the red shaded brain represent a negative mood. I also recorded how well I slept in the morning, and any time I went to the gym to work out.

Creativity

Creativity is extremely important to me. Recently, I’ve found that creativity is more valuable than innovation, because creativity also requires considering the feasibility of an idea. I recorded instances of observing something that I felt was creative or original (indicated with he eye icon). I also recorded any time I felt like I was designing something original from the soul or my intuition (indicated with the lightbulb icon).

Knowledge

I love to learn. I believe where I gather information is important. In order to get a sense of this, I recorded whether I pursued knowledge, and learned something myself, or learned something from someone else.

Stimulation

As someone with ADD, stimulation can often become a distraction. While I have been aware of times I become distracted, I’ve wondered how unproductive distractions impact my mood. I have researched the connection of ADD to depression, and felt that this project was a great opportunity to learn more about the correlation in my own life. Productive stimulation flashes in green, and unproductive stimulation flashes in red.

Final Animation

Reflection

The first thing I noticed was a direct correlation between productive/unproductive stimulation and positive/negative mood respectively. I also found that I am more stressed at night, especially if I’ve spent most of my time with leisure. The third thing I noticed is that I am most creative in the early morning or late at night. Working out and good sleep help my productivity and mood throughout the day. That being said, I realize that I spend very little time working out or resting (being mindful) despite how beneficial both are for my well-being.
